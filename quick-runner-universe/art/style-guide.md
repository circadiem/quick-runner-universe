# Quick Runner Universe — Visual Style Guide

## Core Aesthetic

**Bronze Age superhero comics (mid-1970s to mid-1980s).** Think the era of Kirby's Fourth World, Pérez's Teen Titans, Byrne's X-Men. Bold, confident linework. Dynamic compositions. Characters that radiate energy even in still panels.

## Specific References

- Panel composition and energy: Jack Kirby (Kirby Krackle, explosive perspective, radiating speed lines)
- Character warmth and expressiveness: John Romita Sr. (Amazing Spider-Man era)
- Color palette: period-accurate CMYK printing — warm newsprint tones, limited but punchy color
- Lettering style: hand-lettered look. Sound effects are large, expressive, integrated into the composition — not floating labels

## Panel Conventions

- **Splash panels** for hero moments: full-speed runs, power reveals, dramatic entrances
- **Tight grids (3-4 panels per row)** for dialogue and quiet scenes
- **Diagonal panel splits** for dramatic transitions (used in the origin storm sequence)
- **Sound effects** are part of the art — large, stylized, colored to match the action
- **Speed lines** radiate from the character, not from a vanishing point — Kirby-style energy, not manga-style motion
- **Ben-Day dot shading** on skin tones and backgrounds for period authenticity

## Character Design Notes

### Quick Runner
- Curly brown hair, visible outside the mask
- Green bodysuit, red cape, red boots with lightning details
- Lightning-bolt shaped mask (red)
- "QR" emblem on chest — white letter in a red circle with green background
- Backpack when in civilian clothes (red t-shirt, blue shorts, sneakers)
- Expressive face — wide eyes during action, warm smile during quiet moments

### Wonder Dog
- Cavalier King Charles Spaniel — small, tricolor (black, white, tan/brown markings)
- Green and red harness with prominent "W" on the chest
- Expressive eyes — the dog communicates entirely through expression and body language
- Tail is always doing something: wagging during happy moments, alert and raised during danger
- Small enough to run alongside Quick Runner, never carried

## Image Generation Notes

The origin story panels were generated using AI image generation tools with careful prompting to achieve the Bronze Age aesthetic. Key prompting elements:

- Specify "1970s Bronze Age comic book style" and "newsprint color palette"
- Reference Kirby for energy/composition, Romita for faces
- Explicitly call for Ben-Day dots, bold outlines, hand-lettered SFX
- Avoid modern digital rendering cues — no gradients, no lens flares, no photorealistic lighting
- Each panel should feel like a page from a well-preserved comic from 1978
